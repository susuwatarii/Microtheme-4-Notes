# The Iliad
* Begins in the ninth year of the siege of Troy
* Subject: the wrath of Achilles (quarrel between Achilles and Agamemnon)
* The plot, in brief:
  * Achilles (and his army) refuse to fight for the Greeks
  * The Greeks begin to lose the war
  * Patroclus leads out Achilles' army to help the Greeks
  * Hector kills Patroclus
  * Achilles returns for vengeance, kills Hector

## Book 1
* Achilles vs Agamemnon (first see the WRATH of Achilles)
* Immediate causes: war prizes
  * Plague, Chyseis, Briseis
    * Apollo is the god of plague, sends one upon the Greeks
    * Chryseis required to be returned for plague to end
    * Arete, Chryses
    * Exchange of Arete between Agamemnon and Achilles when Briseis is taken
* Cultural cause: shame vs guilt
  * Win glory, avoid shame
  * Arete
    * How one looks to another
    * Gained by doing great deeds
    * Also gained through spoils and gifts
  * Heroic code?
    * When compromise isn't able to be reached, competition becomes unhealthy
      * Achilles vs Agamemnon

## Book 3
* Duel between Menelaus and Paris
  * Helen (teichoskopia)
    * Feels guilt
    * Teichoskopia = looking out from city wall
    * Trojan men see her and don't look upon her nicely
  * Priam
    * Looks upon Helen with kindness
    * Realizes its all just the Gods being wastemen
    * Only one who understands how both sides aren't evil
    * No perfect resolution to the war
    * One side will perish
  * Aphrodite
    * Whisks away Paris as he's about to die
    * Beautifies him and brings him back to his room
    * Aphrodite goes to Helen as an old woman and says "come on Paris is waiting for you in the bed chop chop"
    * Helen snaps back "you eerie thing where are you taking me, it would be treason to go back to his bed"
    * Aphrodite responds "don't vex me bitch I can make you look repulsive"
    * Helen capitulates
      * Sadge
* First Characterization of Helen:
  * What is her view of the war?
  * Does anybody care?

## Book 6, Glaucus and Diomedes
* Sacred bonds through hospitality (xenia)
* Hector back inside Troy
* Character analysis:
  * Helen and Paris
    * "I wish I were the wife of a better man"
    * "That's no more than Just hector" "I only want to recover from the pain"
    * Heroism in Hector vs Paris
    * Importance of a shaming culture
    * Paris is unconcerned with arete
  * Hector
    * Andromache
      * Shame causes Hector to leave Andromache and defend the city
      * "Show some pity please"
      * "But my shame before the trojans would be too terrible if I hung back by the trojans"
      * He knows what will happen when Troy is sacked, but he can't get shamed if he were to lose, shame propels him
    * Astyanax (his baby son)
      * Hector wishes arete for his son before leaving
      * "Us" is our arete
      * Hector takes on the role of soldier
      * Justifies it through arete

## Book 9

* Agamemnon can't go because of pride so he sends gifts (including Briseis) to Achilles with Odysseus, Phoenix, and Ajax the Greater
* "pls stop being mad"
* All of the heroes have something to relate to Achilles

* Odysseus' speech
  * Achilles hates him because he knows his speech is meaningless; so does Odysseus
    * "It doesn't matter...everybody comes out the same"
  * Two fates of Achilles
    * Either stay and live a long life
    * Or fight and die young
    * "pls stfu Odysseus"
  * Heroic code?
    * Not to be shamed? or to help others?
  * Who is right? Achilles or Agamemnon
  * Cooperation vs Competition
    * Is this good leadership?
    * How should we fight battles

* Phoenix's speech
  * Comes to him as an old man, Meleager
    * After the boar hunt Meleager was upset at her mother because she cursed him to die
    * "I will not fight"
    * "you can have all of these possessions tho"
    * "no"
    * Eventually gets convinced to fight but doesn't get gifts because he was too late
  * "pls put aside your pride" "these possessions will build up your arete"

* Ajax's speech
  * "People are dying idiot, stop whining over a girl"

* The aftermath of Patroclus
  * Patroclus kills Sarpedon
  * Apollo punches his hand away and Patroclus gets wounded

  * Takes away his armor (Armor of Achilles)
  * Achilles laments and eventually everyone does
  * Does end up fighting afterwards
  * Book 18: the shield of Achilles

* Ecphrasis:
  * Literary description of a work of art. Enlivens the inanimate object through description

## Books 20-24, Death and Ransom of Hector
* Athena's role in Hector's demise
  * Hector calls someone for another spear
  * Spear doesn't come
  * Realizes he's going to die because of the gods
* Is this Just treatment by the gods?
  * Probably not
  * Message is that randomness is a big factor in war

* Treatment of the dead
  * Drags Hector around the city walls
  * Apollo rejuvenates his body after every day
  * Zeus eventually puts a stop to it
  * Allow Priam to give ransom and take Hector

* Priam & Ransom of Hector's body
  * Achilles still angry
  * Hector's body lies on the ground beneath his couch

* Achilles sees the world only through anger
* When Priam is at his knees, Achilles has pity
  * "remember your father Achilles... what is left for me?"
  * "I have kissed the hand of the man who has killed my son"
  * Two jars
  * The man that was pulling the corpse around the walls is not the same man that's talking to Priam
  * "This is the human condition"
  * We're both humans
  * One of us is going to have to fall
  * I'll give you your son back
  * Humanistic Achilles

* What do we learn about the character of Achilles in this exchange between him and Priam?
  * Is there a heroic transformation
    * Probably
    * Achilles released his rage since Patroclus died
    * Cooperates with Priam
    * Imagine that he'll cooperate more and more with the Greeks
  * How does the epic end?
    * Reminds us that war is ugly
    * There is no right or wrong side
    * This epic ends on Hector, the breaker of horses, and his funeral
    * Protecting his city
